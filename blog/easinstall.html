L.R. Baggs Element Active Installation

Posted on January 23, 2015

Recently I was installing a L.R. Baggs Element Active pickup system into a Martin Dreadnaught and figured it would be worth while to make a quick post about it, since I recommend and install a fair number of these. Before I give you my opinions about the system though, here is what Baggs has to say about the system.

  • "Unlike typical undersaddle acoustic guitar pickups that are dependent on string compression for their sound, the Element was engineered to mirror the soundboard's actual movement as you play. At the heart of the pickup, a flexible film sensor only 2 thousandths of an inch thick picks up the guitar's most delicate nuances. Tracking the motion of the top rather than a direct string attack suppresses "quack" caused by compression and unlocks the Element's unrivaled dynamic range. The pickup's minimalist construction lessens any influence on the acoustic properties and creates an intimate coupling between the pickup and guitar for the highest fidelity possible."

What I've always loved most about this system is the way it sounds. Relative to a lot of other systems available, this one sounds natural and reproduces a tone that I find to be true to the guitar it's in. It's output is also very dynamic. Regardless of how it's being played, it tends to always give you more of the sound the you want and less of the sound that you don't.

This is also my favorite system to add to an acoustic guitar because it requires very little modification to the instrument. Anyways, here are some pictures of an installed system.
